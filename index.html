Who We Are

The Foundation

The Padma Shri Dr. Shyam Singh Shashi Foundation is a prestigious cultural and institutional organization dedicated to continuing the profound legacy of Dr. Shashi. Our focus encompasses the study and preservation of tribal heritage, the promotion of literature, and extensive socio-anthropological research.

We believe in giving a voice to the voiceless, drawing inspiration from Dr. Shashi's encyclopedic works on Indian tribes, nomads, and marginalized communities.

The Visionary

Padma Shri Dr. Shyam Singh Shashi

A Life Dedicated to the Voiceless

Dr. Shyam Singh Shashi (1 July 1935 – 19 February 2025) was a distinguished Indian socio-anthropologist, author, poet, and scholar. Earning the first Ph.D. from Agra University with a focus on Himalayan nomads, he dedicated his life to documenting India's diverse cultural landscape.

Awarded the Padma Shri in 1990 by the Government of India, his extensive career included serving as Director of the Publication Division (Ministry of I&B), Chairman of the International Institute of Nomadic Studies, and Honorary Editor of the Collected Works of Dr. B. R. Ambedkar.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

Vision

To create a globally connected society that respects, understands, and preserves the cultural heritage of marginalized and nomadic communities.

Mission

To advance socio-anthropological research, promote extensive literary works, and advocate for the welfare and recognition of tribal populations worldwide.

Core Values

Humanity, Inclusion, Academic Rigor, Cultural Preservation, and continuous dedication to elevating the voices of the unseen.

A Journey of Excellence

Milestones

1935

Early Life

Born in Haridwar, India. Later pursued higher education in the UK (University of Manchester).

Ph.D.

First Ph.D. on Nomads

First person to earn a Ph.D. from Agra University with a thesis on Himalayan nomads.

1990

Padma Shri

Awarded the Padma Shri by the Government of India for literature and anthropology.

Publications

Encyclopedic Projects

Authored monumental works including Encyclopedia Indica (150 vols) and Encyclopedia of Indian Tribes.

Legacy

Global Recognition

Served as Chancellor of Int'l Roma University and received the first D.Litt. in Anthropology from Univ. of Colombo.

Life's Work

Key Contributions

Literature

Over 350 books, poetry collections, and massive encyclopedias covering Indian history and culture.

Anthropology

Pioneering ethnographic studies on Himalayan nomads, shepherd communities, and Gypsies.

Social Work

Giving a voice to marginalized groups, advocating for the oppressed castes and recognizing their rights.

Cultural Research

Deep dives into the night life, customs, and dormitory systems of various Indian tribal groups.

Tribal Welfare

Extensive focus on the status and welfare of tribal women through dedicated publications.

Int'l Recognition

Bridging cultures as Chairman of the International Institute of Nomadic Studies.

"Our true wealth lies not in our cities, but in the untold stories and untamed spirits of our nomadic and tribal heritage. To understand them is to understand the very roots of humanity."

— Dr. Shyam Singh Shashi
